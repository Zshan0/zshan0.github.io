Polarization [TO DO]
Similar to the results obtained in the Stern Gerlach experiment, polarization of light also portrays the behaviour of discrete states along with measurement effecting the states themselves.
The light used is a linearly polarized light.
The x polarized light will be:
E=E0x^ cos(kz−ωt)\text{E} = \text{E}_0 \hat{x} \text{ cos}(kz-\omega t)E=E0​x^ cos(kz−ωt)
Light when passed through a x polaroid becomes x polarized light.
When the light is passed through x polaroid and then subsequently through y polaroid, no light appears at the end.
