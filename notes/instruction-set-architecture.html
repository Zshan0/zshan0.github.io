Instruction set architecture
Bandwidth:
The amount of work done in a given time
Latency:
response time between the start and the completion of the work.
Bandwidth growth is almost square of the growth in the latency.
Feature size: The minimum size of a transistor/wire in x/y dimension. the transistor count is determined by the surface area of a transistor.
Power and energy
Required concerns:
Power the system can supply. If the processor needs more than this power there is a malfunction due to voltage drop.
Sustained power consumption. too much consumption leads to heating of the processor, so we need to know what power consumption is sustainable by the power system.
TDP: thermal design power, its the power that can be handled by the cooling system. the supplied power is usually more than this. Failure in proper cooling leads to device failure / permanent damage.
Energy efficiency: Energy depends on a particular task irrespective of how much time it takes to process.
Comparison of efficiency must be done based on the task and the energy it consumes.
Where does the energy go?
For the traditional microprocessors, the energy goes in flipping transistor states, called as dynamic energy.
